Age Appropriate Chores for Kids: The Complete Guide

May 2026 · 6 min read · Parenting Tips

One of the biggest chore mistakes parents make is assigning tasks that are either too easy (boring) or too hard (frustrating). Getting the match right is the difference between a child who feels capable and proud, and one who gives up before they start.

Here's a practical, realistic guide to what children can actually handle at each age — and roughly how long each task should take.

The goal isn't a perfectly clean house. The goal is a child who grows up knowing how to contribute — and feeling good about it.

Ages 2–3: The helper stage

🧸 Toddlers (2–3 years)

  • Put toys in a box or basket
  • Put dirty clothes in the laundry hamper
  • Wipe up small spills with a cloth
  • Help carry lightweight groceries
  • Feed a pet with supervision

⏱ Time: 2–5 minutes per task

At this age, the goal isn't a quality result — it's building the habit of helping. Celebrate every attempt, even if you need to redo it afterwards.

Ages 4–5: Building independence

🎨 Preschoolers (4–5 years)

  • Make their own bed (roughly)
  • Clear their plate from the table
  • Water plants
  • Sort laundry by colour
  • Dust low surfaces with a cloth
  • Help set the table

⏱ Time: 3–8 minutes per task

Ages 6–8: Real contributors

🌟 Early school age (6–8 years)

  • Tidy their bedroom properly
  • Wipe down kitchen counters
  • Load and unload the dishwasher
  • Sweep floors
  • Take bins to the kerb
  • Fold and put away their own laundry

⏱ Time: 5–15 minutes per task

This is the sweet spot for chore games and timers. Children this age are competitive and respond brilliantly to the challenge of beating the clock.

Ages 9–11: Growing responsibility

🏆 Tweens (9–11 years)

  • Vacuum rooms
  • Clean bathrooms (sink, toilet, mirror)
  • Wash dishes by hand
  • Prepare simple meals
  • Mow the lawn with supervision
  • Do their own laundry start to finish

⏱ Time: 10–25 minutes per task

Ages 12+: Near-adult capability

🚀 Teenagers (12+)

  • Cook full meals for the family
  • Deep clean the kitchen
  • Grocery shopping with a list
  • Look after younger siblings
  • Wash the car
  • Manage their own schedule of chores

⏱ Time: 20–45 minutes per task

The most important rule

Whatever age your child is, the single most important rule is consistency. A five-minute daily contribution beats a two-hour Saturday blitz every time. Small, regular habits compound into capable, confident young people.

And when the chore feels like a game — with a spinning wheel to pick it and a countdown timer to beat — even the most reluctant child tends to give it a go.
